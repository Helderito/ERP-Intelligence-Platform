# ADR-0001

# Adopt Modular Monolith with Clean Architecture and Domain-Driven Design

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** 2026-07-04

**Decision Makers:**

- Helder Gonçalves

---

# 1. Context

The ERP Intelligence Platform is intended to become a modern, enterprise-grade business management system capable of supporting multiple business domains, including Inventory, Purchasing, Sales, Finance, Business Intelligence and Artificial Intelligence.

At the beginning of the project, an architectural decision was required regarding the overall structure of the application.

Several architectural approaches were considered, each offering different advantages in terms of complexity, scalability, maintainability and operational requirements.

Because this decision influences every subsequent engineering activity, it has been formally documented as the first Architecture Decision Record.

---

# 2. Problem Statement

The project requires an architecture capable of:

- supporting long-term evolution;
- remaining maintainable as the codebase grows;
- facilitating AI-assisted software development;
- enforcing clear separation of responsibilities;
- allowing future modularisation;
- avoiding unnecessary operational complexity during the early stages of development.

The chosen architecture must balance simplicity with long-term scalability.

---

# 3. Considered Options

The following architectural approaches were evaluated.

## Option 1 — Traditional Layered Architecture

Advantages:

- Simple to understand.
- Widely adopted.
- Fast initial development.

Disadvantages:

- Business logic tends to leak into multiple layers.
- Difficult to maintain as complexity increases.
- High coupling between components.
- Poor scalability for large enterprise systems.

---

## Option 2 — Microservices

Advantages:

- Independent deployment.
- Excellent scalability.
- Clear service boundaries.

Disadvantages:

- Significant operational complexity.
- Distributed transactions.
- Infrastructure overhead.
- Increased development effort.
- Premature optimisation for an early-stage product.

---

## Option 3 — Modular Monolith

Advantages:

- Single deployable application.
- Strong module boundaries.
- Lower operational complexity.
- Easier debugging.
- Simplified deployments.
- Straightforward transition towards microservices if required in the future.

Disadvantages:

- Requires architectural discipline.
- Module boundaries must be actively enforced.

---

# 4. Decision

The ERP Intelligence Platform shall adopt a **Modular Monolith** architecture.

Within the Modular Monolith, all software shall follow:

- Clean Architecture
- Domain-Driven Design (DDD)
- SOLID Principles
- Dependency Injection
- Separation of Concerns

Each business domain shall be implemented as an independent module with clearly defined responsibilities and minimal coupling.

Business logic shall reside exclusively within the Domain layer.

Infrastructure concerns shall remain isolated from business behaviour.

---

# 5. Rationale

This decision was made because a Modular Monolith provides the best balance between engineering simplicity and long-term scalability.

The selected architecture:

- supports incremental development;
- enables clear domain boundaries;
- facilitates AI-assisted development;
- reduces operational complexity;
- supports future modular extraction if required;
- aligns with modern enterprise software engineering practices.

The architecture is particularly well suited to ERP systems, where business consistency is generally more important than independent deployment.

---

# 6. Consequences

### Positive

- Strong separation between business domains.
- Reduced technical debt.
- Easier maintenance.
- Better testability.
- Consistent architecture.
- Improved onboarding of new developers.
- Better support for AI-assisted engineering.

### Negative

- Requires strict architectural governance.
- Developers must understand DDD concepts.
- Module boundaries must be continuously monitored.
- Architectural violations must be prevented through reviews and automated checks.

---

# 7. Implementation Guidelines

The following rules apply to all modules.

- Every module shall expose a well-defined public interface.
- Domain logic shall never depend on Infrastructure.
- Controllers shall remain thin.
- Business rules belong to the Domain layer.
- Database access shall occur through abstractions.
- Modules shall communicate through clearly defined contracts.

Future Architecture Tests shall verify compliance with these rules.

# 9. Alternatives for Future Review

The selected architecture may be reviewed if future business or operational requirements justify a different approach.

Possible future evolution includes:

- Event-Driven Architecture
- Modular Microservices
- Distributed Domain Services

Any future architectural change shall be documented through a new Architecture Decision Record.

This ADR remains valid until explicitly superseded.
